Ermias Andargie Walelgne (D.Sc. (Tech)) holds a Doctoral degree in Networking Technologies from Aalto university. Dr. Ermias successfully defended his doctoral thesis titled Performance and Usage Patterns of Mobile Networks , where his opponent was professor Tommi Mikkonenin from Helsinki University. He has been a Marie Curie Early-Stage Researcher in the Department of Communications and Networking-  Aalto University, Finland. He has been  advised by Prof. Jukka Manner and  Prof. JÃ¶rg Ott. He has also worked with Dr. Vaibhav Bajpai  at TUM.

Currently, he is working at Ericsson as a Cloud & Networking Developer. Before joining Ericsson he worked as a Machine learning Engineer at CGI. He is interested in software development and DevOps focusing on cloud and network automation. He is also interested in internet measurements, mobile data analysis, Machine learning, characterizing wireless network performance and data modeling, user experience, and QoE modeling. The Marie Curie ITN project - METRICS funds his work.

He has been a visiting Ph.D. student at Chair of Connected Mobility at the Technical University of Munich at different times (2017/'18/'19) and   Elisa - Helsinki (2016/7). He has got awarded several student travel grants (CoNEXT'15, IMC'17, NOMS'18, TMA'19, IMC'19), including the successful application for RIPE 77.

He did his BSc degree in Computer Science in 2008 from Hawassa University, Ethiopia. From September 2008 to 2010. He worked as a graduate assistant at University of Gondar .  He has been working at Bahir Dar University, Institute of Technology, as an assistant lecturer from September 2010 to 2011. He studied his MSc degree at University of Trento, Italy, focusing on data management and internet technologies - advised by Prof. Themis  Palpanas. He has been an  intern student from August 2012 to Nov 2012, in the position of Junior Researcher, at University of Illinois Research Center (Advanced Science Research Centre (ADSC)), in Singapore.
